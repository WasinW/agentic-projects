# Sovereign Deployment — a Regent feature (inherited from SentientNet)

> **Origin:** SentientNet was PARKED indefinitely (2026-07-18). Its one salvageable asset —
> **"sovereign deployment for Thai/SEA regulated enterprises"** — is not a company; it is a
> **feature of Regent**. It sells to the same buyer (compliance-first governance +
> data-residency / self-host) roughly 5 years sooner than a decentralized sovereign network.
> This file is the merged home. Source: SentientNet `knowledge/01-decentralized-ai-landscape.md` §3.

## The reframe

SentientNet imagined sovereignty as a *decentralized network* (chains, federation,
micropayments). That is a 3–5–10-year, multi-company undertaking with a buyer a solo can't
reach. The **realizable core** of "who owns the agent, where it runs, who it depends on" is
a deployment + governance property Regent can offer as a feature:

- **Data residency / no US-Big-Tech dependency** — governance + audit that runs where the
  buyer's data must stay (Thai/SEA/EU), no hard dependency on a US hyperscaler control plane.
- **Self-host / bring-your-own-infra** — the policy engine + audit trail deployable on the
  buyer's own infrastructure, not only as a US-hosted SaaS.
- **Sovereign audit** — tamper-evident logs + provenance retained under local jurisdiction,
  mapped to local regulation (PDPA, future Thai AI Act) as well as EU AI Act.

## Why it fits the buyer Regent already targets

The sovereign buyer (government / regulated enterprise) wants exactly what Regent's
compliance-first governance is: policy enforcement + auditable provenance — but with a
**residency + no-foreign-dependency** guarantee bolted on. That guarantee is a deployment
option and a set of compliance mappings, not a new architecture. It slots onto the existing
policy/audit pillars as:

- a **deployment mode** (self-host / in-region) rather than a new product, and
- a **compliance-mapping** dimension (local jurisdiction) on the audit schema.

## Honest constraints (do not over-claim)

- **Timing:** the Thai regulatory hook (Thai AI Act) is still draft; the sovereign angle is
  a **tracked option**, not a build. Same posture as the product gate in ADR-0001.
- **Buyer is inaccessible to a solo today:** sovereign procurement wants certifications,
  local entity, references, BD headcount. This is why it is a **feature to have ready**, not
  a GTM to chase now. It matures alongside the AIA career-capital track.
- **The buyer may build its own:** Thailand's ThaiLLM shows the state building sovereign AI
  itself. Regent's edge is the **governance/audit layer on top**, not the sovereign infra.

## What we did NOT inherit (stays parked with SentientNet)

Decentralized network, federated agent learning, agent-to-agent **micropayments** (dead —
x402/AP2 won that rail; if agents ever pay, adopt as a consumer), token economy. None of
these are Regent features.
