# Content Strategist — Comprehensive Knowledge

> Deep reference for the content-strategist subagent. Senior creator-economy / short-form-video lens, biased toward solo creators building an organic audience from zero. Default context: **Sin's สายมู (Thai spiritual/mystical) AI-art creator business — TikTok-first**, growing toward affiliate + product revenue.

---

## 1. Foundations

### What a Content Strategist does

Owns the **audience-building side** of a creator business: turning attention into followers, followers into a community, and community into revenue. Not a one-off campaign operator (that's marketing-consultant) and not the visual maker (that's ai-art-director) — the strategist owns *what to make, for whom, how often, and why it compounds*.
- Niche + positioning for a creator account (not a brand's CRM base)
- Content pillars, formats, and the editorial calendar
- Short-form mechanics: hooks, retention, watch-time, loops
- Distribution strategy across TikTok / Reels / Shorts (platform-native, not cross-post)
- The creator funnel: reach → follow → engage → convert
- Systemization: batching, repurposing, build-in-public, avoiding burnout

The job is to make sure every post earns attention *and* moves the account toward a compounding audience — not a viral one-off that converts no one.

### Distinct from adjacent roles (read this first)

| Role | Owns | Lens |
|---|---|---|
| **content-strategist** (this) | Audience growth via organic short-form; the creator funnel | "Will this post earn attention + compound a following?" |
| **marketing-consultant** | Enterprise CRM/loyalty; existing customer base → LTV | "Does this move Customers × Frequency × Basket × Margin?" |
| **ai-art-director** | The *visuals* — aesthetic, prompt craft, model choice, consistency | "Does this look intentional, on-brand, and scroll-stopping?" |

The split that matters: **marketing-consultant nurtures a base you already own** (email, loyalty, paid CRM). **content-strategist builds the audience from strangers** on a borrowed-reach platform. ai-art-director makes the asset *look* right; content-strategist decides *what asset, what hook, what cadence* and whether it earns reach. Don't let the strategist drift into pixel-level art notes, and don't let the art director set the content calendar.

### The creator funnel

```
REACH      →   FOLLOW    →   ENGAGE     →   CONVERT
(FYP/strangers) (the decision) (community)   (affiliate/product)
   ↑               ↑              ↑              ↑
 hook +         "more of        comments,     trust +
 watch-time      this" +         saves,        a clear
 + shares        profile         DMs, returns   offer
```

Most new creators obsess over REACH (virality) and neglect the other three. A 100K-view video that gains 12 followers and sells nothing is a **failure dressed as a win**. The strategist's job is to keep all four stages moving — reach is just the top of the machine.

## 2. Mental Models / Decision Frameworks

### Hook → Retention → Payoff (the spine of every short)

Every short-form video is three jobs:
- **Hook (first 1–2s)** — stop the scroll. Visual + text + audio land *simultaneously*. State the stakes or the curiosity gap immediately. No intros, no "hey guys."
- **Retention (the middle)** — earn every second. Cut dead air, use pattern interrupts, withhold the payoff, ride a loop. Watch-time and completion are the #1 ranking signals (§6).
- **Payoff (the end)** — deliver on the hook's promise *and* set up the next action (follow, comment, watch again). A broken promise tanks completion and trains the algo to bury you.

**Opinion:** amateurs polish the middle; pros obsess over the first 2 seconds and the last 2 seconds. The hook decides if you get watched; the payoff decides if you get followed.

### Content pillars (3–5 themes, no more)

Pick **3–5 repeatable themes** the account always returns to. Pillars give the algorithm a clear topic signal, give the audience a reason to follow (predictable value), and give *you* a content engine that never runs dry. For Sin's สายมู account, pillars might be: *daily horoscope/มูเตลู guidance · sacred-object/amulet aesthetics · "AI-art reveal" process · ritual/luck how-tos · personal spiritual story*. Every post maps to exactly one pillar.

### The 3-criteria niche test

A creator niche must pass all three or pick another:

| Criterion | Question | If it fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Affiliate-rich** | Are there products/links the audience will *buy*? | No revenue path — pure hobby |
| **Visually-engageable** | Can it be made scroll-stopping in 1–2s? | Won't survive the FYP |
| **Personal-fit** | Can *you* make this 3×/week for a year without hating it? | Burnout before traction |

สายมู passes all three: rich affiliate inventory (amulets, crystals, candles, courses, divination tools), inherently visual + mysterious (perfect for AI art), and personally resonant for Sin. **This is a strong niche — protect the focus, don't dilute it.**

### Platform-native > cross-post

Each platform rewards different behavior (§6). **Re-cut and re-frame per platform; never dump the same export with a watermark across all three.** A TikTok-watermarked clip is algorithmically suppressed on Reels. The 2026 winning workflow: *create once, adapt per platform* — same core asset, platform-specific hook, captions, aspect framing, and audio.

### Batching > daily scramble

Produce in **dedicated sessions** (e.g. shoot/generate a month of art in one block, write 12 hooks in one sitting, edit in a batch). Batching beats on-demand creation on consistency, quality, and burnout — and consistency is the single biggest lever for a new account (§3, §5).

### Build-in-public

Document the journey — the AI-art process, the wins, the failures, the follower milestones. Transparency builds the parasocial trust that converts (the Levels/Lou/Welsh model, §7). For a สายมู creator: "30 days of building my มู account" or "I asked AI to design the perfect lucky amulet" turns *process* into content and the audience into co-travelers.

### Voice > aesthetic

Beautiful AI art with no point-of-view is wallpaper — it gets scrolled. **The voice (perspective, personality, recurring opinions) is what makes someone follow vs. just like.** Aesthetic earns the stop; voice earns the follow. Sin's actual personality + Thai มู perspective is the moat AI can't copy.

### Value vs. volume

7 mediocre posts/week lose to 3 strong ones — repeatedly confirmed in 2026 data. The algorithm punishes a string of low-completion videos (it lowers your baseline distribution). **Post as often as you can while keeping quality above your own bar — not one post more.**

## 3. Standard Practices

### Short-form mechanics

- **Hook in 1–2s** — the FYP test happens in the first ~2 seconds. Open on the most interesting frame/claim, not a build-up. Layer text hook + visual hook + audio hook together.
- **Design for loops** — for sub-15s videos, aim for *>100% average watch time* (people rewatch). Seamless end→start loops, "wait for it" payoffs, and "watch again to catch it" mechanics inflate watch-time, the top signal.
- **Optimize completion** — in 2026 you need **70%+ completion** to go viral (up from ~50% in 2024); videos hitting ≥85% completion get roughly a **4× distribution multiplier**. Shorter videos complete more easily — don't pad.
- **Earn the strong signals** — shares > saves > comments >> likes. Likes are the weakest signal; engineer *shares and saves* (relatable, useful, "tag someone," save-worthy reference content).

### Posting cadence for a new account

- **3–4×/week, sustained** beats daily-then-quit. Build to daily only if batching makes it sustainable.
- **Consistency > frequency** — the algorithm and audience both reward a predictable rhythm; gaps reset momentum.
- **Realistic growth curve (executing well):** ~500–1,000 followers by day 90, ~3–5K by month 6, ~10–20K by month 12. Anything faster is luck; plan for the curve, not the lottery.

### Content calendar

- Map every slot to a **pillar** (§2) so the mix stays balanced and the topic signal stays clean.
- Plan ~2–4 weeks ahead; keep a **swipe file** of hooks, trends, and sounds to react fast.
- Reserve flex slots for **trend-jacking** (trending audio/format applied to your niche) — these are your highest-reach lottery tickets.

### Repurposing pipeline (1 asset → many)

One core asset (a long video, a carousel, or an art reveal) **atomizes into 5–10+ short clips** and adapts across platforms. The pipeline:

```
1 core asset → 5–10 micro-clips → re-hooked + re-captioned per platform
            → TikTok (native, trend-audio)
            → Reels (trending audio, community-first)
            → Shorts (searchable title, evergreen)
            → carousel / static for IG feed + Pinterest
```

This is how solo creators feed three platforms without 3× the work.

### Hashtags / SEO

- **TikTok/Reels:** a few *specific niche* hashtags + the topic in on-screen text and the spoken/caption copy (TikTok increasingly indexes content as search). Skip giant generic tags (#fyp does little).
- **YouTube Shorts:** treat like search — keyword-rich **title** + description; Shorts surface in search for months/years (the evergreen platform).
- **สายมู SEO:** target what people actually search — "ขอพร...", "เสริมดวง...", "อมูเลต...", specific deity/ritual names.

### Series & formats

Recurring **named series** ("มูประจำวัน", "AI ออกแบบ...") build appointment-viewing and make the follow decision easy. Lock 2–3 repeatable formats so production gets faster and the audience knows what to expect.

### A/B hooks + thumbnails

- Test **one variable** at a time — same video, different first-frame text, or different cover. Read it on completion + watch-time, not likes.
- For Shorts, the **cover/title** is a real lever (search surface); for TikTok, the **first 2 seconds + on-screen hook** are everything.

### Community management

Reply to comments in the **first 60–90 min** (drives the velocity window, §6). Pin a comment that adds a CTA or extends the story. Treat DMs as the warm-lead channel into the funnel. Comments are content prompts — mine them for the next video.

## 4. Tools Landscape (2026)

### Editing
- **CapCut** — the default short-form editor (TikTok/Reels/Shorts). Best-in-class auto-captions, trend templates, effects, AI assists. Free tier covers most solo needs.
- **Blitzcut / Cutback / Klipa** — AI-first mobile editors for fast batch cutting + atomizing long → short.
- **Mobile-native** (TikTok/Reels in-app editors) — fine for trend-audio-native posts; use the native editor when the trend lives inside the app.

### Scheduling
- **Metricool** — scheduling + analytics + best-time recommendations + competitor tracking; strong free tier for a solo creator.
- **Later** — visual planner, scheduling + analytics; solid free tier (1–2 posts/wk/platform).
- **Buffer** — simple multi-platform scheduling.
- **Note:** TikTok rewards *native* posting; for TikTok specifically, posting in-app (or via TikTok's own scheduler) can outperform third-party for reach — schedule Reels/Shorts via tools, post TikTok native when feasible.

### Analytics
- **Native dashboards first** — TikTok Analytics, Instagram Insights, YouTube Studio (Shorts). They show *retention graphs* (where viewers drop) — the single most useful diagnostic.
- **Metricool / Later** — cross-platform reporting + posting-time optimization.
- Watch: **average watch time, completion rate, retention curve, shares, saves, follower-conversion-per-video** — not raw views.

### AI assists
- **Claude / ChatGPT** — hook ideation, caption writing, series planning, repurposing scripts (caption work in *Sin's voice*, not generic).
- **Image/video models** — owned by ai-art-director; the strategist specifies *what* the asset must do (hook frame, format, aspect), the art director makes it.
- **AI editing (auto-caption, auto-cut, b-roll)** — CapCut + dedicated tools to kill the editing bottleneck (the #1 burnout cause, §6).

### Trend tools
- **TikTok Creative Center** — free, official: trending sounds, hashtags, formats by region (filter to Thailand).
- **In-app "trending sounds"** + the Reels audio page — fastest real-time signal.
- **Metricool/posteverywhere-style trend feeds** — supplementary.

**Opinion:** the lean 2026 solo stack = **CapCut (edit) + Claude (copy/ideation) + Metricool or Later (schedule + analytics) + TikTok Creative Center (trends)** — mostly free, covers every layer, no overlap. Don't buy more tools until a specific bottleneck demands it.

## 5. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-pattern | Issue | Better |
|---|---|---|
| Chasing virality over niche | A viral fluke off-topic gains wrong/no followers, confuses the algo | Optimize for *niche-relevant* reach that compounds a real audience |
| Inconsistent / burst-then-quit posting | Momentum resets; algo deprioritizes; audience forgets you | Sustainable 3–4×/wk via batching, indefinitely |
| No hook / slow intro | Lose viewers in the first 2s; completion craters | Open on the most interesting frame; hook in 1–2s |
| Aesthetic whiplash | Random looks confuse the topic signal + the brand | Lock a consistent aesthetic system per pillar (with ai-art-director) |
| Vanity metrics (views, likes) | Don't equal followers or revenue | Track watch-time, completion, saves/shares, follower-per-video, conversion |
| Cross-posting watermarked exports | TikTok-watermarked clips get suppressed on Reels/Shorts | Re-cut + re-hook platform-native |
| Selling before trust | Hard CTA to cold reach converts nobody, kills reach | Earn trust first (value + voice), then a clear soft offer |
| One giant pillar / no focus | Algo can't categorize; audience can't predict value | 3–5 tight pillars, each post mapped to one |
| Ignoring the retention graph | Flying blind on *why* videos die | Read drop-off points; fix the exact second viewers leave |
| Trend-jacking with no niche bridge | Reach without relevance = junk followers | Only ride trends you can twist back to สายมู |
| Over-producing / perfectionism | Slow cadence, burnout, fewer at-bats | "Good + posted" beats "perfect + next month" |
| Posting then ghosting comments | Misses the velocity window + community building | Reply in first 60–90 min; mine comments for content |
| Buying followers / engagement | Wrecks the engagement ratio the algo reads | Earn real reach; ratio integrity matters more than count |

## 6. Advanced / Expert Topics

### Algorithm mechanics per platform (2026)

| | **TikTok** | **Instagram Reels** | **YouTube Shorts** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Discovery bias** | Most aggressive to *strangers*; content > follower count | Favors your *existing* audience + similar-interest users first | Hybrid: recommendation feed **+ search** |
| **Top signals** | Watch-time, completion, **view velocity** (≈30-min window), shares | Watch-time, saves/shares, **trending audio**, account history | Watch-time, search relevance, topic/channel consistency |
| **Content lifespan** | Ephemeral — peaks in 48–72h (occasional late spikes) | Short-to-medium | **Evergreen** — surfaces in search for months/years |
| **Best for** | Fast viral discovery from zero | Engaging + converting your community | Long-term searchable visibility |

**Implication for Sin:** **TikTok-first for cold reach** (build the audience fast), **Shorts for evergreen สายมู search demand** (people search horoscopes/rituals for years), **Reels to deepen + convert** once a community exists. Don't split effort evenly — lead with TikTok, repurpose to the other two.

**TikTok distribution pipeline:** initial test (~200–500 views) → expansion (1K–50K) → viral breakout (100K+). A **~30-minute view-velocity window** decides whether you graduate to broad FYP — which is *why* the first hour of engagement (your reply window) matters so much.

### Growth loops

The compounding engines, in order of leverage:
1. **Save/share loop** — save-worthy + shareable content spreads peer-to-peer *and* signals the algo. Engineer "tag someone who needs this luck" / reference posts.
2. **Series loop** — named recurring formats create return viewers → higher baseline distribution.
3. **Profile→binge loop** — a strong video sends people to your profile; a coherent grid + pinned best work converts the follow + a binge (more watch-time).
4. **Comment→content loop** — answer comments with new videos; the audience co-writes your calendar and feels ownership.

### Creator → product funnel

```
TOP (reach)      MIDDLE (trust)        BOTTOM (convert)
viral/niche  →   story, how-to,    →  affiliate link, TikTok Shop,
hooks            "why this works",     own product/course,
                 build-in-public       clear single CTA
```

TikTok affiliate content averages ~5%+ engagement (~160% above comparable IG), and TikTok Shop / YouTube Shopping let creators tag products with in-app checkout — **own the funnel end-to-end, don't send cold traffic off-platform.** For สายมู: top = lucky/divination hooks; middle = the meaning + ritual + your story; bottom = the amulet/crystal/course link. Most creators jump straight to bottom-funnel and convert no one — earn the middle first.

### Multi-aesthetic portfolio strategy

A single account should hold a **consistent core aesthetic** (the topic/brand signal). If Sin wants to explore distinctly different visual worlds (e.g. dark-mystical vs. soft-pastel มู), run them as **named series within one account** *or* spin separate accounts — never blend randomly on one feed (aesthetic whiplash, §5). Decision rule: separate accounts only when audiences + monetization genuinely diverge; otherwise one account, multiple series. Coordinate the aesthetic system with **ai-art-director**.

### Parasocial / community

The follow is emotional, not rational. **Recurring voice, face/persona, in-jokes, and replying personally** build the parasocial bond that converts far better than any hard sell. For สายมู this is amplified — the audience wants *guidance and belonging*, not just pretty images. Cultivate the community (replies, rituals-together, milestones) as the real product; the affiliate revenue follows trust.

### When to go paid / ads

**Organic first — earn product-market-fit for your content before spending.** Go paid only to: (a) **amplify a proven organic winner** (a video already converting), or (b) accelerate a validated bottom-funnel offer. Never pay to fix weak content — paid reach on a bad hook just buys expensive proof it doesn't work. For a new creator, the answer is usually "not yet."

### Creator burnout / systemization

~62% of creators report burnout in 2026 (relentless cadence + algo churn + ~100:1 demand-supply gap). Defenses:
- **Batch everything** — separate ideation / generation / editing / scheduling into blocks.
- **Atomize** — one core asset → 5–10 posts, so output ≠ effort linearly.
- **Automate the boring** — AI captions, scheduling, repurposing scripts.
- **Diversify income** — affiliate + product + (later) services, so no single algo change is existential.
- **Boundaries + buffer** — keep a 1–2 week scheduled backlog so a bad week doesn't break the streak. **A burned-out creator posts zero; a systemized one posts forever.**
